## Project Profiles

### 1. Bittensor (TAO)

- **Chain:** Bittensor (Substrate-based L1)
- **Price:** $223.93 | **Market Cap:** $2.47B | **FDV:** $4.7B
- **Circulating Supply:** 11.03M / 21M max

**Description:** Decentralized machine learning marketplace where miners train/run ML models and validators evaluate their quality. Uses a novel "proof-of-intelligence" consensus called Yuma Consensus.

**Tokenomics:** Bitcoin-like halving schedule — 50% of TAO rewards are cut every 4 years. 21M max supply mirrors Bitcoin's scarcity model. Miners and validators earn TAO for ML contributions.

**Architecture:** Substrate-based blockchain with dedicated TAO L1. Miners serve ML inference/compute. Validators rank miner performance. Complex subnet architecture for specialized tasks.

**Differentiator vs WattCoin:** Broader AI/ML marketplace vs WattCoin's focused compute-for-bounties model. Much higher market cap and liquidity, but less targeted at individual gig workers. Higher technical barrier to participate.

**Community:** 318K followers | **Launch:** 2021

**Strengths:** First-mover in decentralized AI, strong Bitcoin-like tokenomics, active research ecosystem, largest market cap in sector
**Weaknesses:** Validator centralization risk, complex tokenomics, high barrier for miners, speculative without clear product-market fit

## Strategic Positioning

### Key Market Gap WattCoin Fills

Most existing protocols either:
1. Require **significant technical expertise** (Bittensor miners, Akash container deployment)
2. Focus on a **narrow niche** (Render = 3D rendering, Nosana = inference only)
3. Target **enterprise rather than individuals** (Aethir, Akash)
4. Deal with **data rather than compute** (Ocean Protocol)

WattCoin's **compute bounty marketplace** model is unique in combining:
- Low barrier for workers (anyone with a CPU/GPU)
- Flexible task types (rendering, ML training, inference, data processing)
- Asynchronous execution (post bounty → worker completes → verification → payment)
- Solana's low fees and fast settlement

### Competitive Threats

1. **Nosana**, if it expands beyond pure inference into general GPU tasks
2. **Akash**, if it builds a simpler user-facing layer on top of container deployments
3. **Render**, if it continues its expansion into AI compute bounties

### Unique Advantages

- **Human verification** via proof-of-completion (more flexible than automated systems)
- **Global workforce** — opens compute markets to developing-nation participants
- **Task diversity** — not locked into any single AI/rendering paradigm
